First-author paper · 2022

Mechanism of artificial surface defect induced cracking for very high cycle fatigue of Ti alloys

Jian Sun, Wenjie Peng, Chengqi Sun. Engineering Fracture Mechanics 272 (2022) 108721.

A drilled surface defect does not reproduce the slow internal-crack process normally associated with very-high-cycle fatigue. By tracking a TC17 specimen to final failure and then resolving the mated crack surfaces, this work showed that most of the life was spent before an observable crack appeared; once initiated, the crack propagated rapidly. The case study connects macroscopic S-N behavior with crack-tip microstructure.

01 · Research context

Why this problem matters

TC17 titanium alloy is used for aero-engine compressor blades, where machining damage or service-induced surface flaws can act as fatigue origins. Very-high-cycle fatigue (VHCF), conventionally associated with lives beyond 10^7 cycles, is especially difficult to assess because internal initiation can involve an extremely slow early-growth stage and characteristic rough-area or fine-granular features.

Artificial surface defects are often used to study defect tolerance, but their long-life failure process is not automatically equivalent to internal-defect-induced VHCF. Earlier observations had shown a plateau-like S-N response and no obvious rough area around surface defects. The unresolved question was whether a surface defect produced a similarly slow initiation process, or whether a different time-dependent mechanism delayed crack formation.

02 · Scientific question

Core question

What physical process allows a TC17 specimen with an artificial surface defect to survive for tens of millions of cycles, yet fail soon after a crack becomes observable?

03 · Materials & methods

Connecting loading history to local evidence

The study linked loading history, direct crack observation, fracture morphology, and local crystallography rather than relying on an S-N curve alone.

Material

Beta-forged TC17; solution treated at 800 °C for 4 h, water cooled, and aged at 620 °C for 8 h.

Mechanical state

Yield strength 1061 MPa; tensile strength 1145 MPa; basketweave lamellar alpha microstructure.

Artificial defect

A drilled defect on the polished plate specimen: average width 332 µm and depth 157 µm. Measured sqrt(area) for failed specimens was 197-217 µm.

Fatigue loading

USF-2000A ultrasonic system at 20 kHz, stress ratio R = -1, continuous loading, with compressed cold air for temperature control.

Observation

In-situ optical monitoring, SEM fractography, and EBSD of polished sections and mated crack surfaces.

Focused condition

Detailed mated-surface analysis at stress amplitude 368 MPa and failure life 9.61 × 10^7 cycles.

04 · Experimental design

Designing the comparison

The defect was placed at the center of the polished gauge section, making it possible to observe the same prospective initiation site during testing. Failed specimens covered high-cycle and VHCF lives. An unbroken specimen was also loaded into the VHCF regime and sectioned at two depths beneath the surface to test whether hidden cracking or grain refinement had already developed near the defect.

05 · Key findings

Evidence and interpretation

01

A plateau-like S-N response emerged in the VHCF regime.

At lower stress amplitudes, defect-containing specimens still failed after very long lives, while the stress amplitude no longer showed a clear downward trend with increasing life. The authors interpreted this as evidence of a critical-stress-type response for the studied surface defect, distinct from the continuously descending trend often reported for internal initiation.

02

All observed failures initiated at the artificial surface defect, without a surrounding rough area.

SEM showed defect-originated fracture in both high-cycle and VHCF specimens. No fine-granular area or rough area was found around the drilled defect. That morphology matters: it indicates that the slow internal-crack initiation and early-growth process was not reproduced at the artificial surface defect.

03

The observable crack formed only near the end of life.

For the specimen monitored in situ at 368 MPa and a failure life of 1.95 × 10^7 cycles, the paper reports that the interval from the first visible crack to a very large surface crack was less than 1 s - fewer than 20,000 cycles at 20 kHz. This directly supports comparatively rapid visible crack growth near final failure, rather than a crack that propagates slowly throughout most of the measured life.

04

Nanograins and extension twins were associated with local crack-tip deformation.

EBSD of mated crack surfaces revealed small grains, including nanoscale grains, and deformation twins in alpha grains at crack tips or near crack surfaces. Their location, together with elevated KAM values, connected these features to local high stress and plastic deformation during crack growth - not to a long nanograin-assisted initiation stage at the original defect.

06 · Failure mechanism

A staged interpretation

The paper proposes a two-timescale interpretation. The environmental examples below are hypotheses discussed by the authors, not separately isolated variables in the experiment.

01

Long pre-crack interval

Cyclic loading acts at the surface defect for most of the measured life without a detectable propagating crack.

02

Time-dependent assistance

The authors attribute delayed crack formation to a synergy between cyclic stress and time-dependent processes, citing moisture or hydrogen as possible contributors.

03

Crack initiation

Once the coupled conditions become sufficient, a crack initiates at the artificial defect.

04

Rapid propagation

The crack grows quickly relative to the total life; local high stress produces twinning and grain refinement near advancing crack surfaces.

05

Final failure

The fracture lacks the rough-area signature of the slow internal VHCF initiation route.

08 · Impact & future direction

What this work enables next

Research significance

The study cautions against treating all defect-originated VHCF failures as the same process. For defect-tolerant assessment, total life cannot necessarily be inferred from a conventional crack-growth picture when most cycles precede detectable crack formation. The work therefore motivates models that separate pre-crack incubation from subsequent propagation.

Boundaries of the evidence

The proposed time-dependent assistance was inferred from morphology and timing; moisture and hydrogen were not independently controlled in this test program. The drilled defects occupied a narrow size range, and the mechanism should not be generalized to other titanium microstructures, environments, defect shapes, or loading frequencies without further validation.

Doctoral research direction

A natural doctoral extension is to combine controlled-environment VHCF testing with microstructure-sensitive or crystal-plasticity models around quantified surface defects. Probabilistic incubation models could then be linked to short-crack growth, providing a more defensible defect-tolerant life framework.
