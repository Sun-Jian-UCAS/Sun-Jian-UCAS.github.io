I am a Master's graduate in Engineering Mechanics from the **University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS)**, Institute of Mechanics, advised by Prof. Chengqi Sun. My research focuses on the fatigue and fracture behavior of metallic materials, with an emphasis on titanium alloys and high-strength steels.

I have investigated **very high cycle fatigue (VHCF)**, **dwell fatigue**, the influence of **defects and notches** on fatigue strength, and the underlying **microstructural mechanisms** using SEM and EBSD characterization. I am currently seeking **PhD positions** to further my research in mechanics and materials science.

## Research Interests

- Very high cycle fatigue (VHCF) and high cycle fatigue (HCF)
- Dwell fatigue and time-dependent deformation in titanium alloys
- Fatigue crack initiation and early growth mechanisms
- Effects of defects, notches, and surface conditions
- Microstructure-property relationships via EBSD and SEM
