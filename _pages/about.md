# Jian Sun

I am a Master's graduate in Engineering Mechanics from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS), Institute of Mechanics, where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Chengqi Sun. My research is centered on the fatigue and fracture behavior of metallic materials, with a particular focus on titanium alloys and high-strength steels. I have investigated very high cycle fatigue (VHCF), dwell fatigue, the influence of defects and notches, and the underlying microstructural mechanisms using advanced characterization techniques such as SEM and EBSD. I am currently actively seeking PhD positions to further explore the mechanics and materials science of structural alloys.

## Education

- **M.S. in Engineering Mechanics**, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Institute of Mechanics (Sep 2020 – Jun 2023)  
  Thesis: *Mechanism of Artificial Surface Defect Induced Cracking for Very High Cycle Fatigue of Ti Alloys* (co-supervised)
- **B.S. in Engineering Mechanics**, Lanzhou University of Technology (Sep 2013 – Jun 2017)

## Research Interests

- Very high cycle fatigue (VHCF) and high cycle fatigue (HCF)
- Dwell fatigue and time-dependent deformation in titanium alloys
- Fatigue crack initiation and early growth mechanisms
- Effects of defects, notches, and surface conditions on fatigue strength
- Microstructure-property relationships using EBSD and SEM
