This is the personal academic website of **Jian Sun**. I am a Master's graduate in Engineering Mechanics from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS), Institute of Mechanics, under the supervision of Prof. Chengqi Sun. My research centers on the fatigue and fracture behavior of metallic materials, with a particular focus on titanium alloys and high-strength steels. I have investigated very high cycle fatigue (VHCF), dwell fatigue, the influence of defects and notches, and the underlying microstructural mechanisms using advanced characterization techniques such as SEM and EBSD. I am currently actively seeking PhD positions to further explore the mechanics and materials science of structural alloys.

Research Interests
------
- Very high cycle fatigue (VHCF) and high cycle fatigue (HCF)
- Dwell fatigue and time-dependent deformation in titanium alloys
- Fatigue crack initiation and early growth mechanisms
- Effects of defects, notches, and surface conditions on fatigue strength
- Microstructure-property relationships using EBSD and SEM
