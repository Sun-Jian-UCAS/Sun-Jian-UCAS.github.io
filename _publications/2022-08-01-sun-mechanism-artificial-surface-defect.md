This paper explores the very high cycle fatigue (VHCF) behavior of TC17 titanium alloy with artificial surface defects. Through in-situ observation of crack growth and EBSD characterization, we demonstrate that the artificial surface defect induced VHCF failure presents no slow process of crack initiation and early growth accompanied with nanograin formation—commonly observed for internal cracking induced VHCF. The failure mechanism is attributed to the synergistic effect of cyclic stress with time-dependent processes (e.g., moisture influence, hydrogen action) during long-term cyclic loadings. Nanograins and deformation twins were also observed in α grains at or near crack surfaces.
