This study examines the effects of notches and defects on the dwell fatigue behavior of Ti-6Al-4V ELI alloy, a material critical for deep-sea submersible pressure hulls. We found that dwell fatigue is insensitive to defect size (190–438 μm) compared to conventional fatigue. For notched specimens, dwell fatigue life was higher than that of smooth specimens at the same local maximum stress, and the dwell of maximum stress had no influence on fatigue life or failure mechanism. Facet features observed in the crack initiation region were attributed to fracture of α grains, with fine line markings corresponding to steps on the fracture surface.
