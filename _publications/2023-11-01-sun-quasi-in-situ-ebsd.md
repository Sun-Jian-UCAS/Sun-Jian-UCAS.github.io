This paper presents a novel quasi in-situ EBSD observation method combining conventional MTS testing with EBSD characterization to investigate microstructure and damage evolution in Ti-6Al-4V ELI alloy. Key findings include: (1) Slip tends to occur in α grains with larger Schmid factors for basal or prismatic slip; (2) Twinning occurs in α grains with Schmid factors ≤ 0.2 for prismatic slip and c-axis angles < 46° to the loading direction; (3) Subgrain formation due to twinning and dislocation sliding was captured during cyclic loadings. The study provides insight into how maximum stress dwell and dwell time influence crack nucleation and damage evolution at the microstructural scale.
