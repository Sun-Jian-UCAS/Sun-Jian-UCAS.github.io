This study investigates the effects of natural aging and discontinuous cyclic loading on the high cycle fatigue (HCF) behavior of 25CrMo4 and 30CrMnSiA steels. Long-term natural aging (> 20,000 h) reduced fatigue life for both steels, attributed to surface corrosion and hydrogen action. The effect on failure mechanism was microstructure-dependent: natural aging promoted multi-site crack initiation in 25CrMo4 steel but had no effect on the crack initiation mode of 30CrMnSiA steel. Discontinuous cyclic loading had no harmful influence on fatigue life or failure mechanism, with both continuous and discontinuous loading resulting in single-site or multi-site crack initiation at the specimen surface.
